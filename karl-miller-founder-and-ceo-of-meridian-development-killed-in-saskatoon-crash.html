Karl Miller, Founder and Ceo of Meridian Development, Killed in Saskatoon Crash

Deadly crash kills family of four; CEO of Meridian Development, Karl Miller, died in a crash along with his wife and two children.

Karl Miller, founder and CEO of Meridian Development, died in a deadly crash along with his wife and two beautiful children on Sunday night, October 15, 2023, in Saskatoon, Canada. All four were pronounced dead at the scene, leaving the community shocked and mourning. Our thoughts and prayers are with the family.

What happened to Karl Miller?

According to the Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP), four people were killed in a deadly crash between an SUV and a semi on Sunday night northeast of Saskatoon on Highway 41near Aberdeen.

The fatal accident occurred around 7:45 p.m. CST near the intersection of Edinburgh Road. All four occupants of the SUV were pronounced dead at the scene. The semi’s driver did not report any injuries to the police.

The RCMP did not release the identity of the victims, but according to a local news channel, the president and co-founder of Meridian Development, Karl Miller. His wife and their two children were killed in the accident.

Colleen Wilson, Miller’s business partner and friend, confirmed the news. Wilson told the media that Miller’s next of kin had been notified.
The RCMP is investigating the incident to learn more about the circumstances which led to the accident.

Who was Karl Miller?

Karl Miller was co-founder and CEO (Chief Executive Officer) of the award-winning company Meridian Developer. He was well-renowned in the construction industry. His innovative ideas blended architecture with the urban landscape while maintaining sustainability earned him the reputation of leading developer and Meridian many accolades. He has worked on prestigious, high-profile, unique projects that have become valuable assets in the community and also graced low-profile initiatives equally with his experience, such as the development of a community skating rink in collaboration with Affinity Credit Union, His immense knowledge was founded in his academic endeavours. He had a degree in Commerce and Urban Planning from the University of Saskatchewan. He was invited to speak at civic forums and participated in the Saskatchewan Real Estate Forum. He was a proud recipient of the Young Alumni Achievement Award by the University of Saskatchewan. He demonstrated professional esteem with the expansion of Meridian’s operations to British Columbia, establishing his company’s future growth with attention-grabbing projects.

Obituary and funeral

His family will release his obituary and provide details of his funeral at a later date. They are currently healing from the devastating loss of an entire family. All are requested to respect their mourning and allow them the time to grieve with respect.
We offer our deepest condolences to the family and friends. May they find comfort in this crucial time.
