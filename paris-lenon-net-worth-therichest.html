American professional football player Paris Lenon has an estimated net worth of $10 million.

Paris Lenon started playing football while attending the Heritage High School where he served the free safety and quarterback positions. He was named as theWestern District Defensive Player of the Year. In college, he was a four-year letterman and three-year starter for the Richmond Spiders. During his stint with the team, he played 45 games, 320 tackles, 12 sacks, and four forced fumbles.

He was undrafted free agent during the NFL Draft in 2000. Lenon signed for the Carolina Panthers but was released by the team on June 9, 2000. He had a brief stint with XFL's Memphis Maniax, Green Bay Packers and Seattle Seahawks in 2001. Few months after his stay for the Packers, he re-joined the team on December 27, 2001.

Other teams that Lenon played with include Detroit Lions, New England Patriots, St. Louis Rams, Arizona Cardinals, and Denver Broncos. He also returned for the Lions in 2013 after his stint with the team in 2006.

Paris Michael Lenon was born on November 26, 1977 in Lynchburg, Virginia. He is married to Heather Vanden Heuvel and has three children.
