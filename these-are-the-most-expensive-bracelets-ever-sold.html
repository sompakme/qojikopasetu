The Magnificence Of The M. Gérard Diamond Bracelet

Highly Important Diamond Bracelet by Gerard

In 1968, Louis Gérard launched his own company, M. Gérard, after resigning from his position as a manager at the upscale jewelry retailer Van Cleef & Arpels. Gérard was regarded as France's top exporter of exquisite jewels during the height of his fame. Even though the company went out of business in December 1991, its diamonds are still quite expensive, according to TheMostFamousList. For example, Christie's sold an M. Gérard diamond bracelet with two rows of diamonds and a 29.01 carat diamond in the center at auction in 2006. Over $1.5 million was paid for the bracelet at auction.

Wallis Simpson Cartier Panther Bracelet Is The Miraculous Armlet One Needs

American socialite Wallis Simpson married Prince Edward, who famously ceded his kingdom to wed the twice-divorced Simpson. Moreover, it is the most expensive piece of Cartier to ever be sold at auction.
