Early Life

Rasheeda Widad Buckner, widely known as Rasheeda, was born on May 25, 1982, in Decatur, Georgia. Her early life laid the foundation for her successful career as a rapper and entrepreneur.

During her teenage years, Rasheeda formed the hip hop group Da Kaperz in 1998, together with two of her friends. This marked the beginning of her musical journey and provided her with valuable experience in the hip hop industry.

Da Kaperz signed a deal with D-Low Entertainment, but Rasheeda’s ambition and drive eventually led her to pursue a solo career. This decision proved to be a turning point in her life, propelling her towards greater heights of success.

Rasheeda’s early experiences in the music industry and her decision to break away from the group paved the way for her future accolades and accomplishments. Her determination and talent set the stage for a remarkable journey filled with milestones and recognition.

Music Career

Throughout her career, Rasheeda has made significant strides in the music industry, releasing several albums and collaborating with renowned artists. Her solo career took off in 2001 with the release of her first album, “Dirty South,” under Motown Records. The album garnered attention and her debut single, “Do It,” became a minor hit, establishing her as a rising talent.

In 2002, Rasheeda continued to make waves with her second solo album, “Ghetto Dream.” This project featured collaborations with popular artists like Lil Jon and the Eastside Boyz, further solidifying her presence in the music scene. The album showcased her versatility as an artist and showcased her ability to deliver captivating performances.

Following the success of “Ghetto Dream,” Rasheeda released her third album “GA Peach.” This album showcased a different side of her artistry, featuring slower, sexier tracks that resonated with her fan base. The hit single “My Bubblegum” catapulted her into the spotlight as a breakout star, gaining widespread recognition and cementing her status as a prominent figure in the industry.
