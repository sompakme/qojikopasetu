Joshua Jacksons $8 Million Fortune At Risk With No Prenup Amid Jodie Turner-Smith Divorce

Highlights

  • Jodie Turner-Smith and Joshua Jackson didn't sign a prenup before their 2019 wedding, potentially leading to a costly separation for Joshua due to his larger net worth.
  • Jodie isn't seeking spousal support, but Joshua could be responsible for her legal fees considering his significantly higher net worth.
  • Jodie filed for divorce citing irreconcilable differences and requested joint custody of their daughter, Juno. The couple will co-parent and want their daughter to thrive.

Jodie Turner-Smith and Joshua Jackson shocked fans earlier this month with the revelation that their marriage is coming to an end after more than three years. But the separation could become complicated, as reports allege the couple didn’t sign a prenup before their 2019 wedding. And with a significantly larger net worth, Joshua could potentially be paying big for the split.

As per court filings obtained by Daily Mail, Jodie acknowledged they don’t have a prenup in place, but she’s not requesting spousal support for either her or her ex.

It has been two decades since the show ended and the stars then have certainly grown up now.

Known for his role in the teen drama Dawson’s Creek, Joshua reportedly has a net worth of $8 million. Jodie’s is half his at $3 million.

Daily Mail reports the couple live in Joshua’s former childhood home, located in Topanga, California, which he purchased in 2001. He previously earned a West Hollywood home with Diane Kruger, though they sold it for a little under $6 million in 2017 following their split. Joshua went on to date Jodie shortly after.

“My father unfortunately was not a good father or a husband and exited the scene, but that house in Topanga was where everything felt simple, so it was a very healing thing for me to do,” Joshua later said of his decision to buy his childhood house.

Though Jodie isn’t seeking spousal support, as per her filing, that doesn’t mean Joshua won’t be on the hook for her legal fees, considering his more significant net worth.

The model has reportedly hired celebrity divorce lawyer Laura Wasser, who’s helped negotiate the million-dollar divorce settlements of celebrities like Kim Kardashian, Angelina Jolie, Heidi Klum, Johnny Depp, and more recently, Kevin Costner.

Jodie filed for divorce on October 1, citing “irreconcilable differences.” She requested joint custody of their daughter, Juno, born in 2020. “She decided that she is done,” a source told PEOPLE. "They are on very different paths in life. Jodie loves being a mom. She also loves working."

"It turned into an unhealthy marriage that made her unhappy,” they continued. “They will co-parent their daughter. They want her to continue to thrive."

Joshua and Jodie first sparked break-up rumors in 2022 when they unfollowed each other ons social media, though they seemed to reconcile shortly after by re-following each other. Recent reports say that Jodie’s decision to file for divorce has taken Joshua off-guard, but he’s chosen to not publicly discuss it yet.
