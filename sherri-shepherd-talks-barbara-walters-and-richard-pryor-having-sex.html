Sherri Shepherd Talks Barbara Walters And Richard Pryor Having Sex

Celebrity relationships often drive conversations on social media and in publications, especially ones that are unexpected. Sherri Shepherd recently reflected on finding out that Barbara Walters and Richard Pryor were involved with one another at one point.

The actress welcomed her former The View co-host Joy Behar onto her eponymous talk show on Wednesday (Oct. 11), and the two shared a laugh about how Behar can’t hold water. “I never told this before, I told Joy that I had run into Paul Mooney, and Paul Mooney had said that he caught Barbara Walters with Richard Pryor,” Shepherd said. Behar asked for confirmation on the two having relations, to which Shepherd repeated that the deceased Meet The Blacks actor said so.

Evidently, Mooney had walked in on Pryor and Walters having sex, and Shepherd told Behar. Behar wasted no time asking Walters about the situation one day when they all showed up to do an episode of The View. “She turned around, and you know, Barbara’s head would swivel… She goes, ‘Who told you that?!'” Shepherd said. “I’m sitting there, like I had just got this job. And Joy, you [did] that because you’re not scared of Barbara!”

Joy Behar and Sherri Shepherd continued the conversation, laughing about how Barbara Walters had an affinity for Black men. “Between you and me, Barbara liked a brother,” Behar said. “She was going out with a Black senator — Is this gonna be on television?!” Shepherd added, “You always used to say Barbara loved a chocolate man.”

Sherri Shepherd recently underwent a transformation by getting breast reduction surgery and dished on a unique plan for her old bras that involves Drake.

“I found out that Drake is collecting bras that were thrown at him and he’s gonna auction ’em off,” she told ET, in reference to a photo of The 6 God with all of the bras women have thrown at him during the It’s All A Blur Tour. “So I’m sending him a care package… And he’s gonna be happy. These are grown women’s bras, Drake. These are not 22-year-old women bras. These are not [from] them women going, ‘Oh my God, you’re so great!’ These are grown woman bras with good credit, OK?”
