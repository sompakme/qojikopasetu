The Real Reason Stassi Schroeder Was Fired From Vanderpump Rules

Longtime reality TV hit, Vanderpump Rules, has been entertaining audiences for years now. The show has done just about everything right while on TV, and, maybe most importantly, it has always known who to focus attention on when the cameras are rolling.

Stassi Schroeder was a mainstay on the show for years, and her meltdowns are the stuff of legend. Stassi was ultimately fired from the show, and some people aren't entirely in the loop as to why she was booted. She's doing well now, but people still want to know what she was let go from the hit reality show.

Let's take a closer look at Stassi Schroeder's time on Vanderpump Rules, and learn why she was given the boot from the hit show.

Vanderpump Rules Is A Wildly Popular Reality Show That Has Been Around For Years

Vanderpump Rules has become one of the most popular reality shows on TV, and it's easy to see why. The show follows the lives of the staff of the West Hollywood restaurant SUR, owned by the incomparable Lisa Vanderpump.

With a unique cast of interesting personalities, Vanderpump Rules has become a hit with audiences everywhere. The show first premiered in 2013, and was created by Lisa Vanderpump and her husband, Ken Todd. They wanted to create a show that would show off the dynamic and drama of the staff at SUR, and it ended up being an instant success. It has become one of Bravo's highest-rated programs, and is a network mainstay that people still can't get enough of.

The main cast of Vanderpump Rules has consisted of a number of longtime employees at SUR, and it's easy to see why they've become so popular. The show follows the lives of Stassi Schroeder, Jax Taylor, Scheana Shay, Tom Sandoval, Ariana Madix, Katie Maloney-Schwartz, Kristen Doute, James Kennedy, and Lala Kent.

Each of these people have helped make the show a worthy watch for reality TV fans, and the show's success can largely be attributed to them.

Of course, when looking at the show's biggest names, it's impossible to ignore Stassi Schroeder.

Stassi Schroeder Made A Name For Herself Thanks To Landing A Spot On The Show

Stassi Schroader has come a long way in her career, and she took a most unexpected journey to get to where she is today.

According to Hollywood Life, "She got her big break as a teen when she appeared with her family on Season 8 of The Amazing Race, then suffered a huge loss when her family’s home was destroyed in Hurricane Katrina. She moved to Los Angeles shortly after, and starred in the reality show Queen Bees while attending Loyola Marymount University, where she eventually earned a Bachelor’s degree in English Writing. In 2013, Stassi got her second big break when her waitressing job at Real Housewives of Beverly Hills star Lisa Vanderpump’s restaurant SUR led to a spot on the reality spinoff series Vanderpump Rules."

Talk about being in the right place at the right time!

It seems like a pretty typical thing for reality shows to keep their biggest and most notable stars around for as long as they can, but this show sent shockwaves in the reality TV community when it was announced that Stassi would not be returning.

Why Stassi Was Fired From Vanderpump Rules

So, why in the world was Stassi Schroeder let go from her hit reality show?

"One week after former “Vanderpump Rules” cast member Faith Stowers revealed that two of the show’s original cast members, Stassi Schroeder and Kristen Doute, had reported her to the police for a crime she’d had nothing to do with, a Bravo spokesperson confirmed to Variety that Schroeder and Doute won’t return to the show," Variety reported.

That's a lot to chew on. What took place between those individuals was serious business, and clearly,Stassi and Kristen went about handling things was all wrong.

When this announcement was made, those two weren't the only stars being dismissed from the show.

"Additionally, new cast members Max Boyens and Brett Caprioni, whose past racist tweets were uncovered after the most recent season premiered in January, also will not return," the report continued.

If you thought that Stassi's career was over, well, you'd be wrong. Since being removed from the show, the former reality star has been quite busy. She's been caught up with writing and podcasting.

"Stassi Schroeder Clark is the author of New York Times Bestsellers Off with My Head (2022) & Next Level Basic (2019)," her website states. It also mentions that her podcast "recently relaunched in 2022, after a 2-year hiatus."
