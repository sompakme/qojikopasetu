The BJ Novak Way: Here's How Ryan From 'The Office' Makes His Millions

The multi-talented satire artist is known to wear various professional hats. Novak’s claim to fame is the eight-year-long comedy-drama The Office (2005), for which he also shares credit as the executive producer and writer. His passion, however, remains to be stand-up comedy since he began his career performing comic gigs in Los Angeles clubs. Making a quiet directorial debut in 2022, Benjamin wrote and directed the dark thriller Vengeance, which grossed an estimated $4.4 million at the global box office. He also starred in blockbuster films, including Inglorious Basterds, The Amazing Spider-Man 2, and Crazy Ex-Girlfriend. Setting up a quirky-themed restaurant with chef Tim Hollingsworth and producing shows for television have become lucrative long-term deals for the famed comedian. Let us look at his highly successful journey as an actor and entrepreneur.

B.J. Novak took a paycheck of $20,000 per episode as an actor during the debut of The Office. After season 4, he received a raise to $100,000 per episode, estimated at $2.8 million per season during the final seasons.

Stand Up Comedy

Novak debuted as a stand-up comedian in Los Angeles clubs in 2001. However, some of his early gigs failed to impress the audience. He then took a three-month break and booked five shows in a row after returning to the comic gig scene. According to NPR, while performing on a regular gig at the Hollywood Improv, Greg Daniels, the creator of the hit comic sitcom The Office, spotted him and offered him a career-defining role in the popular series.

His Acting Career In Television

Playing the arrogant Ryan Howard on the hit NBC comedy sitcom, The Office gave Novak instant recognition in the television industry. He also worked as a writer and executive producer of the show. The Office earned the seasoned comedian two Primetime Emmy award nominations and honored him with two Screen Actors Guild awards. According to Stylecaster, he took a paycheck of $20,000 per episode as an actor during the first season of the sitcom. After season 4, he received a raise to $100,000 per episode, estimated at $2.8 million per season during the final seasons. He has also starred in other television productions like The Newsroom (2012), The Premise (2021), and the reality show Punk’d (2003).

An Established Movie Star

Novak has starred in blockbuster hits like Inglorious Basterds (2009) opposite Brad Pitt, and the film collected $321.4 million at the global box office. He was also featured in Amazing Spider-Man 2 (2014), and the film made an estimated $709 million globally. Staying true to his comic roots, Benjamin was featured in Knocked Up (2007), which collected $219.9 million globally.

A Published Author

Novak is the author of a widely successful children's book called The Book With No Pictures. He often tours the country, reading the book to kids. His short stories book One More Thing is also highly recommended in the readers market worldwide.

Novak's Directorial Debut

According to The Things, in 2022, Novak received rave reviews for his first-time-directed crime thriller Vengeance. The film collected $4.4 million at the global box office. He directed the feature, wrote the story, and starred in it.

His Ventures As A Producer

Novak is credited as an executive producer for various television projects, such as The Mindy Project, starring his best friend and co-star from The Office, Mindy Kaling. He has also written and produced an anthology series for Hulu titled The Premise.

Owning A Pop-Up Restaurant

According to Hollywood Reporter, Novak, in collaboration with star Otium chef Tim Hollingsworth launched Chain on 7th March 2021. It is a uniquely whimsical WeHo pop-up restaurant that reconstructs gourmet versions of takes on dishes from TGIFridays, Chili’s, and California Pizza Kitchen. Based in Southern California, the sold-out menu items drop exclusively on Chain’s Instagram account and must be ordered and paid for in advance. The invite-only multimillion-dollar restaurant is the favorite haunt of Hollywood A-listers, influencers, and wealthy kids. A merchandise shop also sells sweatshirts, t-shirts, bucket hats, bumper stickers, ashtrays, lighters, sauces, and seasoning salts bearing the Chain logo.

His Investment In Tech Apps

In 2015 the Premise actor invested and co-founded a tech app known as the List App. The project was created by developer Dev Flaherty. The app was based on simple functionality. Users could make lists of anything and everything. The app allowed 350 characters, and users could comment on other displayed lists. Favorite lists could be re-listed and shared on Facebook and Twitter. During its debut week, 150,000 users signed up to create around 250,000 lists on the app. The makers re-branded the app into list in 2016 with an estimated $2 million in seed funding. However, in the current scenario, it has been shut down.

B.J.Novak possesses Midas touch and wears all his professional hats with elan. The $10 million net worth is certainly a result of his maverick entrepreneurial and entertainment skills.
